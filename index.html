Research Interests

01

My interest in AI and ML didn't begin with a textbook — it began with a question: can a machine learn to see what a doctor sees? That question drove my undergraduate years at Jyothy Institute of Technology, and it has never stopped driving me since.

02

I am not interested in algorithms for their own sake. I care about what they can do for people — detecting skin diseases in a rural clinic, flagging diabetic retinopathy before a patient goes blind, helping a language model understand a grandmother speaking Kannada. Tangible impact on real lives is the north star of my research.

03

At IIIT-Hyderabad, I am investigating the failure modes of Large Language Models in regional Indian languages — Tamil, Kannada, and others left behind by the mainstream AI race. We are building native evaluation frameworks and augmenting LLMs with multimodal signals.

04

Beyond NLP, I am drawn to Graph Convolutional Networks, Agentic AI, Simulation-Augmented ML and physics-based algorithms that give models a grounding in how the physical world actually works.

05

Weeks of debugging messy real-world datasets for DermAI taught me something no course could: research is not about elegance, it is about persistence. That IEEE publication at ETCC 2025 was not a milestone — it was a beginning.
